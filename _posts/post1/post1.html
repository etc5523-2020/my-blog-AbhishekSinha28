Blog 1: COVID-19 trends from Germany

A look into the coronavirus statistics in Germany!

Abhishek Sinha (ETC5523, Monash University)
09-04-2020

Prelude

On Jan 27, 2020, the Bavarian Health and Food Safety Authority, Germany, was informed of the first human case of infection with SARS-CoV-2 in a German national working for a company in the greater Munich area(Merle M Bohmer 2020). Since then, Germany has been battling the Coronavirus pandemic. In this blog, we take a further look into the magnitude of the country’s Covid-19 numbers.

Data Description

The data is obtained from R package coronavirus(???) provided by RamiKrispin, which contains COVID-19 data from 188 countries or regions, and the raw data collected and arranged by the Johns Hopkins University Center for Systems Science and Engineering (JHU CCSE). In this blog, I will focus on China data, which contains 22,440 observations from 2020-01-22 to 2020-08-28.

Analysis

To start with the analysis the Figure 1 will help you visualize the total deaths and cases in Germany till August 31, 2020. It is also interactive, so you adjust the dates to get a better understanding of how the numbers change across different time periods.

Figure 1: COVID Cumulative Cases and Deaths in Germany

To further understand the rate of new cases the Table 1 helps us to find the rates of confirmed cases, active cases, recovered cases and death from January to August in 2020. This table gives us an overview of how the situation changed in Germany from the time of the first case in January. We can notice that the rate of the confirmed cases increased drastically till April. It is then when Germany implemented strict lockdown as a measure to contain the spread of virus and thus we can see a decline in new cases. We can see that mortality rate has been kept low by Germany. THe confirmed cases rate also indicates that more people are being tested at early stages in Germany helping patients to recover.

Figure 2: COVID cases in Germany

The rates of corona virus cases in Germany over time is displayed in Figure 2. From this analysis we can find a clear trend. This trend can give us an idea about the approach taken by the German government and how successful are those measures. We can see that the cases started to rise very quickly in Germany. To put a stop on this situation Germany implemented lockdown as a measure to contain the spread of virus and thus we can see a decline in new cases. This figure shows us how a strict lockdown helped Germany to contain the spread of virus which can be noticed through stringency index as well.
